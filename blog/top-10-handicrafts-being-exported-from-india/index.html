Top 10 Handicrafts Being Exported from India

Posted on 20/03/201931/05/2020 by Kamran Khan

Top handicrafts being exported from India

India has been well known for its rich culture and heritage. Each culture has its own customs cherished by the people. Such a vast heritage and integration can be found nowhere in the world. Yet such diversity lets us stay united and share some of our renowned richness around the globe.

Handicrafts are one such example of spectacular creations from the hands of skilled individuals spread across the country proving India to be an exotic land with exquisite artworks. Each state is famous for some of the other craft but the Tamil Nadu, Saharanpur, Gujarat, Rajasthan, Kashmir are some centres well acknowledged for handcraft manufacturing.

Handicrafts from India

Artefacts that are crafted with passion and excellence with very less input from machinery’s along with a considerable element of tradition are termed as handicrafts. And India has given a fine share of importance to the creation and export of such artefacts. Though there are innumerable collections of work being manufactured, some notable ones have been globally exported.

The exports trends in India is rising each year. From the data produced by the Export promotion council for Handicrafts (EPCH), exports in India initially started with the year 1986-1987 and till 2016 period the export of handicrafts from India has shown a massive improvement suggesting the Importance and admiration this has gained over the world within the past 35 years.

Export culture from India

Exports and imports are the integrity of a country. It shows how well a county is paying importance to the country’s culture and heritage. Handicrafts apart from being a skill is a proper source of income for the small scale industrialists and women population of the country. The sector of handicrafts paves way for many benefits like

  • A work method for many small scaled rural and urban population aiding the weaker segments of the country.
  • Poses a decent employment potential for skilled people of the country.
  • High output is obtained for the meagre investments to be made.
  • Higher value addition to the products.

With this, get yourself edified on some of the topmost handicraft products that are been exported from India. The selected products are ranked from highest values in export figures according to the data* from ministry of textiles, government of India.

  1. Art metalware

Intricate craftsmanship is invested in bringing out fine work of metal wares. Usually, metals like gold, silver, brass, copper are used to carving out idols, make jewellery, precise utensils. This is a very broad class with several sub-categories like bidri work, bell metal etc.

Moradabad, Orissa is very well known for embellished metal works and products. Shimmering shiny utilities are manufactured at this part of India and exported around the globe and generate a considerable share of annual turnover.

  1. Woodware

A major share of the export market is taken up by the wooden furniture category. Some regal rich designs are well in demand in foreign countries. Woodwares are intricately worked and passionately crafted to precision, and that too with hand!

The wood ware markets are established in various parts of the country like Andhra Pradesh, Delhi, Ahmedabad, Jamnagar, Surat, Gujarat, Bihar, and Ghaziabad. This industry employs numerous workers and is a source of living in rural parts of India taking up making a share of an exported item from the country.

  1. Textile hand printing

If you have a look around your place, it would be interesting to note the immense popularity of hand printings in India. In the land where intricate works of Madhubani paintings, bandhani originated, you would be surprised about how fine the painting works have been accomplished on the fabric.

Some important areas that undertake this level of work are Machilipatinam of Andhra Pradesh, Varanasi, Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat, Maharashtra and Jodhpur of Rajasthan. Infusing natural dyes, block printings are undertaken.

  1. Textile hand embroidery

This sector embarks upon the file layer of threads that are embossed upon fabrics. Depending on the skill sets various types of hand embroidery styles are proficient in India. Every style has its own texture differences, pattern differences and embellished with a craft that is carried along with legacies.

The craft is beautiful, spanning as clusters or well spaces floral design, idol works, mandalas and many more pattern filled fabrics. Some of the prevalent ones are chikankari, zardozi, kashidakari spanning intricate styles over delicate cotton and silk fabrics, progressive over are Bareilly, Lucknow, Bengal, Kashmir and Gujarat.

  1. Handloom shawls

Shawls and other handloom shawls that are unique masterpieces. Statistics conclude that globally India shares around 85% of production. One major reason for this is due to the expansive collection of fibres and yarn types that supports the rich handloom sector.

Handloom shawls and outfit industry has two types of looms requirement, hand weaving and power looms. Handloom has found to be the second major employment generator apart from agriculture. This art is famous around Bhuj, Varanasi, Nawan and other places of India.

  1. Zari and zari goods

Zari works are usually evoked with a fine line of gold or silver yarns. These are traditionally used in garments of silk and velvet. Intricate patterns are embroidered with high craftsmanship. The zari worked material are widely used in garments and home decoration items.

Surat is the most famous location that spins yarns of zari in clothing accounting for 55% of production needs. Apart from them are the places like Vadodara, Bareilly, Varanasi, Agra Hyderabad and Lucknow catering to all inland and export requirements.

  1. Imitation jewellery

One of an artistic handicraft demanding heavy patience is imitation jewellery making. For women to flaunt in style this industry tirelessly works to make beautiful pieces of traditional and modern style jewellery. Most of the simple raw materials used are metal, glass, lac and stones.

There are various levels in classifications of goldsmiths and diamond processing experts in the industry. It is employed mostly in Delhi, Kohima, Mysore, Jaipur and Nizamabad.

  1. Leather footwear

The leather industry is another major sector employing a large number of employees in this traditional art that India is well known for. India has previous records of manufacturing a healthy number of shoes, slippers and footwear with customised designs.

There is Central Leather Research Institute (CLRI) in Chennai that caters to intense leather research. The major manufacturing centres of leather footwears are Chennai, Ranipet, Agra, Lucknow, Jalandhar in Punjab, Haryana, Kolkata, Jodhpur in Rajasthan, and Kerala.

  1. Carpets and rugs

One prominent and age-old industry is the rug and carpet industry continuously focussing on exports. There are different types of hand woven carpets being manufactured throughout the country. Yarn and fibres are the major raw materials used to give a cosy carpet feel filled with rich designs and patterns.

It is believed that each pattern has its own meaning. It is mainly manufactured around Mirzapur, Varanasi, Andhra Pradesh, Gwalior, Agra and Jaipur are some major centres.

  1. Stone carvings

Another age-old art from ancient India is the stone carving industry. It is the process of removal of a predefined amount of stone material. It requires a very high skill set to create masterpieces of stone carvings.

It is usually made for decorative needs of customers, idols, candle stands, jewellery boxes are some of the most commonly made items. The major art crafting places in India like Agra, Bhubaneshwar, Puri, Mysore, Rajkot, and Gwalior.

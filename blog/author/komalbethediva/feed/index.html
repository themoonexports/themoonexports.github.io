History of using horns rimmed eyewear

 

Right from the start horns have perceived bold look to the wearer. It all started when a famous comedian Harold Lloyd started using horn-rimmed spectacles at a play in 1917. Since then the popularity of these horn-rimmed glasses has been instantly prevalent and acclaimed worldwide. During the early ’90s when wearing spectacles itself was defamed and stigmatized, Lloyd used spectacles to portray an ordinary human being which instantly august and well received. After this, horn-rimmed glasses was a trend imposing identity.

As new improvements to the heavy glass materials for frames took positions, and stricter poaching and hunting restrictions imposed, use of horn-rimmed glasses surged deep down. Metallic frames took hold in the buyer’s stands being more economical also. Later, incorporating synthetic horn materials started gaining popularity. In recent days, horns from dead animals are used to adorn items. With classic styles duly making a necessary comeback, so is horn-rimmed glasses.

How is a horn-rimmed glass manufactured?

There are an extensive skill and capacity requirement to carve out the designed frame out of a natural animal horn. It is priced for the luxury it is. Horn-Rimmed eyewear involves several stages of cleaning, polishing and carving out the structure. Special methods have to be included in giving details to the frame. Each piece of the frame may be similar in design, but it’s very unique with the pattern.

For an even surface, the horn piece undergoes numerous traditional polishing and patterning. Colour pallets are chosen from natural animal horn pieces and skinned. The selected piece is sliced, sketched, sharpened. All the processing requires heavy great experience, skill and patience. But again it’s worth the price.
